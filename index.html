About the Laboratory

The Distributed Systems and Control Laboratory (DSCL) focuses on the analysis, design, and coordination of distributed and networked control systems. Our approach integrates systems and control theory with optimization, graph theory, and cybersecurity to address fundamental challenges in autonomous and interconnected systems. While our core strength lies in rigorous theoretical research, we actively translate these ideas into practical solutions through experimental validation and real-world cyber-physical platforms. DSCL is a research group within the Department of Electrical Engineering at IIT Jodhpur. Our team includes undergraduate students, graduate researchers, and collaborators from leading institutions across India and abroad. The laboratory provides an intellectually stimulating environment dedicated to advancing secure, scalable, and resilient control architectures for emerging technologies. We are always looking for highly motivated students and researchers to join our group.

Core Research Areas

Cooperative Guidance and Control

Development of distributed control algorithms for autonomous vehicles and robotic systems functioning in uncertain and dynamic environments.

Formation Control of Multi-Agent Systems

Coordination strategies enabling decentralized robotic networks to achieve desired formations and collective behaviors.

Security in Cyber‑Physical Systems

Design of resilient control architectures and secure communication frameworks to safeguard CPS against adversarial threats and information leakage.

Optimization and Control of Passive Systems

Energy‑aware distributed optimization and control techniques for systems constrained by physical and structural limitations.
